TITLE:
Blanquette de Veau

SPECIAL EQUIPMENT:
- Large pot
- Strainer
- Saucepan
- Whisk
- Frying pan

INGREDIENTS:
- 1 kg of veal (shoulder, collar …)
- 3 L of water
- 2 sprigs of fresh thyme
- 1 fresh bay leaf
- 4 carrots
- 2 onions
- 1 leek
- 3 cloves of garlic
- 1 lemon
- 10 sprigs of fresh chives
- 300 g of Paris mushrooms
- 1 tbsp of sunflower oil
- 60 g of butter
- 60 g of flour
- 20 cl of liquid cream
- Coarse salt
- Pepper
- Espelette pepper

INSTRUCTIONS:
1. In a large pot, blanch the veal, starting with cold water.
2. While the meat is blanching, peel all the vegetables.
3. Cut the carrots in half if they are large, the onions into quarters, the leek in half, and remove the germs from the garlic cloves.
4. Remove the impurities from the broth.
5. Add the vegetables, bay leaf, and thyme.
6. Add coarse salt and pepper.
7. Squeeze half a lemon (be careful with the seeds).
8. Let it cook for about **1h30min** on low heat.
9. Strain to separate the broth from the vegetables and meat.
10. Make a roux in a saucepan or a high-sided frying pan.
11. Only mix the flour and melted butter, do not cook it any further (we are not trying to make a brown roux).
12. Turn off the heat and incorporate some of the broth into the sauce, adding it in small quantities to avoid lumps.
13. Mix well, it will thicken, cook on low heat, it should remain very coating.
14. In another saucepan, reduce the rest of the broth.
15. Quarter the mushrooms on a bias.
16. Incorporate the reduced juice into the sauce to enhance the taste.
17. Whip the cream for **5min** to make whipped cream.
18. Add lemon juice to tighten the cream.
19. Add Espelette pepper, salt, and pepper to the sauce.
20. Incorporate the whipped cream and mix well.
21. In a hot frying pan, heat the oil.
22. When the pan is hot, sear the mushrooms, season with salt and pepper, and caramelize them (there's no need to cook them for too long).
23. Add the pieces of meat and mushrooms to the sauce.
24. Cut the cooked carrots into large beveled pieces.
25. Chop the chives and add them to the sauce along with a zest of lemon.
